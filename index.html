Legendary Games of the 90s

Super Mario World

1990 SNES

The defining platformer of the 16-bit era. Mario and Luigi venture through Dinosaur Land to rescue Princess Toadstool from Bowser, discovering secret exits and hidden worlds along the way.

Fun Facts
  • Introduced Yoshi to the franchise
  • Contains 96 total exits to discover
  • Was the SNES launch title in Japan

The Legend of Zelda: A Link to the Past

1991 SNES

Link must traverse the Light and Dark Worlds to defeat the evil wizard Agahnim and rescue the seven descendants of the Sages in this top-down action-adventure masterpiece.

Fun Facts
  • Introduced the Master Sword to the series
  • Featured a dual Light/Dark World mechanic
  • Sold over 4.6 million copies worldwide

Sonic the Hedgehog

1991 Genesis

Sega's blue blur races through Green Hill Zone and beyond at blazing speed to stop Dr. Robotnik from capturing animals and collecting Chaos Emeralds.

Fun Facts
  • Created to rival Nintendo's Mario
  • Designed by Naoto Ohshima and Yuji Naka
  • The game's speed was revolutionary for its time

Street Fighter II

1991 Arcade/SNES

The game that defined the fighting genre. Eight world warriors compete in one-on-one combat using unique special moves, combos, and Hadoukens that became part of gaming culture.

Fun Facts
  • Pioneered the competitive fighting game genre
  • Had multiple updated versions (Turbo, Super, etc.)
  • Generated over $10 billion in revenue

Doom

1993 PC

A space marine fights through hordes of demons on Mars and in Hell itself. This groundbreaking first-person shooter popularized the genre and introduced network multiplayer deathmatch.

Fun Facts
  • Pioneered the shareware distribution model
  • Created by id Software's John Carmack and John Romero
  • Has been ported to nearly every platform imaginable

Donkey Kong Country

1994 SNES

Donkey Kong and Diddy Kong swing through jungles, ride mine carts, and blast from barrel cannons to recover their stolen banana hoard from King K. Rool and his Kremling army.

Fun Facts
  • Used pre-rendered 3D graphics on a 2D console
  • Developed by Rare using Silicon Graphics workstations
  • Saved the SNES from early retirement

Chrono Trigger

1995 SNES

A group of adventurers travel through time from 65 million B.C. to 2300 A.D. to prevent a global catastrophe. Multiple endings and a "Dream Team" of developers created RPG perfection.

Fun Facts
  • Created by a "Dream Team" including Hironobu Sakaguchi and Akira Toriyama
  • Features 13 different endings
  • Introduced the New Game+ concept

Resident Evil

1996 PlayStation

S.T.A.R.S. members Chris Redfield and Jill Valentine investigate a mysterious mansion filled with zombies, puzzles, and the sinister Umbrella Corporation's biological experiments.

Fun Facts
  • Coined the term "survival horror"
  • The infamous "Jill Sandwich" line became a meme
  • Spawned one of gaming's biggest franchises

Crash Bandicoot

1996 PlayStation

A genetically enhanced bandicoot spins and jumps through tropical islands to defeat the evil Dr. Neo Cortex. Became PlayStation's unofficial mascot and a beloved platformer icon.

Fun Facts
  • Developed by Naughty Dog before Uncharted fame
  • Internally nicknamed "Sonic's Ass Game" for its camera angle
  • The first game to use a PlayStation development technique called "paging"

Pokemon Red & Blue

1996 Game Boy

Catch, train, and battle 151 creatures across the Kanto region to become a Pokemon Master. The link cable trading system created a social phenomenon that transcended gaming.

Fun Facts
  • Creator Satoshi Tajiri was inspired by collecting insects
  • The original 151 Pokemon included the secret Mew
  • Became the best-selling RPG franchise of all time

Final Fantasy VII

1997 PlayStation

Cloud Strife and AVALANCHE fight to save the planet from the Shinra megacorporation and the enigmatic Sephiroth in this cinematic RPG that brought JRPGs to a global audience.

Fun Facts
  • Cost approximately $45 million to develop
  • Spanned three PlayStation discs
  • Contains one of gaming's most memorable plot twists

GoldenEye 007

1997 N64

James Bond infiltrates enemy bases in this revolutionary console FPS. Its 4-player split-screen multiplayer defined countless sleepovers and friendships throughout the late '90s.

Fun Facts
  • Developed by a team with almost no FPS experience
  • The split-screen multiplayer was added near the end of development
  • Oddjob was universally banned among friend groups

StarCraft

1998 PC

Three distinct races - Terran, Protoss, and Zerg - battle for dominance in a sci-fi real-time strategy masterpiece that became a national sport in South Korea.

Fun Facts
  • Became an esport in South Korea with televised matches
  • Sold over 11 million copies worldwide
  • The three races are considered perfectly balanced

Metal Gear Solid

1998 PlayStation

Solid Snake infiltrates Shadow Moses Island to neutralize a nuclear threat by rogue special forces in this cinematic stealth-action game that redefined narrative in gaming.

Fun Facts
  • The Psycho Mantis battle broke the fourth wall by reading your memory card
  • Directed by Hideo Kojima as a passion project
  • Featured fully voiced dialogue, unusual for the era

Half-Life

1998 PC

Theoretical physicist Gordon Freeman fights for survival after a catastrophic experiment at the Black Mesa Research Facility opens portals to an alien dimension.

Fun Facts
  • Told its entire story without cutscenes
  • The mod community created Counter-Strike from its engine
  • Won over 50 Game of the Year awards

The 90s Timeline

1990

Super Mario World

The SNES launches in Japan with Mario's greatest 2D adventure. The 16-bit era begins.

1991

The Console Wars Heat Up

Sonic the Hedgehog arrives on Genesis. Street Fighter II ignites the arcade fighting craze. Zelda returns on SNES.

1992

Mortal Kombat

Mortal Kombat sparks controversy and eventually leads to the creation of the ESRB rating system.

1993

Doom Changes Everything

id Software releases Doom, popularizing the FPS genre and online multiplayer gaming forever.

1994

PlayStation Arrives

Sony enters the console market. Donkey Kong Country stuns with pre-rendered 3D graphics on SNES.

1995

RPG Golden Age

Chrono Trigger sets the bar for JRPGs. The PlayStation gains momentum worldwide.

1996

A Landmark Year

Pokemon mania begins. N64 launches with Mario 64. Resident Evil invents survival horror. Crash Bandicoot debuts.

1997

Final Fantasy VII

FFVII brings JRPGs mainstream. GoldenEye 007 defines console FPS. The PlayStation dominates.

1998

The Greatest Year in Gaming

Half-Life, Metal Gear Solid, StarCraft, Zelda: Ocarina of Time. A year many consider the best in gaming history.

1999

The Decade Closes

The Dreamcast launches, foreshadowing the next generation. The 90s leave behind an unmatched legacy of innovation.

About This Era

🎮

The Console Wars

Nintendo vs. Sega defined the early '90s, pushing both companies to create legendary games. Sony's PlayStation later disrupted the entire market.

💾

From 2D to 3D

The decade saw gaming's biggest technological leap. Sprites gave way to polygons as the N64 and PlayStation ushered in the 3D revolution.

🎧

Iconic Soundtracks

From Koji Kondo's Mario melodies to Nobuo Uematsu's Final Fantasy orchestrations, 90s game music became art that stands on its own.

🔗

Birth of Online Gaming

Doom's multiplayer, Quake's online servers, and StarCraft's Battle.net laid the groundwork for the connected gaming world we know today.
